I.Classical Cryptography

Classical cryptography refers to the traditional methods of encrypting and decrypting messages before the digital age. These methods usually involve simple substitution or transposition of letters and are mostly manual. They are the foundation of modern cryptography but are relatively easy to break with today‚Äôs computers.

I.I. Caesar Cipher

  "One of the earliest and most well-known forms of cryptography was developed by Emperor Julius Caesar. During times of war, he needed a way to send secret messages to his generals. His solution was simple but effective: shift each letter in the alphabet by three positions. For example, the letter A becomes D, B becomes E, and so on. This technique came to be known as the Caesar Cipher."

Source: caesarcipher.net

  Emperor Julius Caesar made his messages secret by shifting each letter three positions forward in the alphabet. The algorithm to encrypt a message works like this:

  1. 1. Assign numbers to each letter:

  2. 2. Convert the plaintext into numbers:
      For example, if the plaintext is WELCOME, we convert each letter to its corresponding number:
    WELCOME ‚Üí 22 04 11 02 14 12 04
  3. 3. Encrypt the numbers by adding 3 to each:
      22 04 11 02 14 12 04 ‚Üí
    (22+3) (04+3) (11+3) (02+3) (14+3) (12+3) (04+3) ‚Üí

      25 07 14 05 17 15 07 ‚Üí ZHOFSQH

  So, the ciphertext of the message WELCOME using Caesar cipher with shift k = 3 is:

Ek=3 (WELCOME) = ZHOFSQH

  To decrypt the message, we simply reverse the process by subtracting 3 from each number and taking modulo 26 again.

II.RSA

RSA is a modern encryption method that allows secure communication over the internet. Unlike classical ciphers, RSA uses mathematics with very large prime numbers to make messages secure.

II.I. RSA Cryptography

RSA Cryptography is a modern encryption technique that uses two keys‚Äîa public key to encrypt messages and a private key to decrypt them‚Äîso that communication stays secure.

  "RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is a widely used asymmetric encryption algorithm that relies on the mathematical properties of prime numbers. It was invented in 1977 by Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman. RSA is based on the difficulty of factoring large composite numbers into their prime factors."
